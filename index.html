How each round works — Biased Dice Rules
A
Sensor Layer: Teacher announces RED or GREEN. A students roll dice. 🟢 GREEN: 3–6 = FIRE (1), 1–2 = silent. 🔴 RED: only 6 = FIRE, 1–5 = silent. They shout 0 or 1 to their B partner.
B
Hidden Layer: B students listen to their connected A nodes, write down inputs, then roll their own dice using the same rule. They shout their result (0 or 1) to their C node.
C
Output Layer: C students receive B outputs. They select RED or GREEN based on majority, convert to numeric (GREEN=1, RED=0), multiply by their weight, and report the value to the teacher.
∑
Decision: Teacher sums all C values. If Σ ≥ threshold → 🟢 GREEN. After a wrong answer, C node weights are adjusted up or down.
